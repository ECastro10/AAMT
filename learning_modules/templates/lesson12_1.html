Sixth Chords

Alright, let's build on our triads to make some interesting sounding chords.
It is worth noting that 6th chords are not traditional in the sense of how we have been building
chords. Let's dive into why and how 6th chords are built.

Major 6th

We will discuss to basic types of 6th chords, the major 6th and the minor 6th.
There are more types of 6th chords out there but we will focus on the basic types.

Let's build a 6th Major Chord.
FORMULA: 1 - 3 - 5 - 6


The reason that 6th chords are not traditional is because it breaks the pattern of thirds.
As you can see, the sixth scale degree does not stack directly on top, if 6ths kept to tradition,
the sixth note would stack on top of the first three notes quite nicely, but those are 7ths which we will
discuss in the next chapter.
Use the scale degrees and the formula to get any Major 6th chord.

Minor 6th

FORMULA: 1 - b3 - 5 - 6


Overall, 6th chords sound interesting but they do not occur naturally in chord families. In the next chapter
we will discuss the 7th's chord family to prove our point.
