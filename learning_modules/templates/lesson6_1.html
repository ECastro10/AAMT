Intervals

Before we go into constructing chords, let's go over intervals.
When we talk about intervals we are usually referring to the space between notes.
Remember why the circle of fifths/fourths is named? That was an example of intervals. When going clockwise
your next key will be a fifth higher, if going counter clockwise it will be a fourth.
The smallest space or interval between two notes is a semitone == to one fret.
Here is a chart with all the names of the intervals.


If 1 fret == 1 semitone, that means that that the octave of a note is 12 frets away (the octave being on the 12th fret).
Next is a couple of staves with the all the intervals on them.


So all of these spaces between notes, up to the same note itself have a name, cool, so what?
These intervals are characterized by sound difference. That is to say that each interval sounds different.
I highly recommend that you practice your intervals and try playing both notes together to hear what sounds good.
If you can't remember all of the names, that is fine, for the time being just remember the followig
Major 2nd
Major 3rd
Perfect 4th
Perfect 5th
Major 7th
