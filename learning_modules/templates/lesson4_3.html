Bar lines, Repeat Marks, and Time Signatures

Bar lines

NOTE: One "|" symbol means the end of a bar, two "|", == "||" means the end of the song.
This helps the reader or writer understand where in the bar he/she is or whether the song should end or not.
This brings us to the other marks on the staff, repeat marks

Repeat Marks

Time Signatures

Let's discuss some time signatures and how to use them

4/4 time

So in 4/4 time (aka common time), one bar is made up of four quarter beats. An auditory way of understanding this is
to count out loud up to four and repeat. NOTE: BOLD text when showing how a time signature works
means to put emphasis on that word, this is known as a stressed beat. You don't have to stress any beats if you don't want to.
So you would count "One - Two - Three - Four | One - Two - Three - Four || <-- This is == 2 bars
You can do this for as long as you like, the result is counting four beats per bar.
I only stressed the first beat to show you where the bar begins, but you can stress any or all beats if you like.
The note duration is important because it gives us a feel for what the time feels like.
4/4 time == 4 beats per bar/quarter note beats

2/2 time

2/2 time or c with a | through it == cut time should sound like this:
One - Two | One - Two ||
2/2 time == 2 beats per bar/half note beats

2/4 time

One - Two | One - Two ||
2/4 == 2 beats per bar/quarter note beats
It is important to note that even though my 2/2 and 2/4 examples look the same, the note durations differ.

3/4 time

One - Two - Three | One - Two - Three
3/4 time == 3 beats per bar/quarter note beats

6/8 time

One - Two - Three - Four - Five - Six | One - Two - Three - Four - Five - Six ||
6/8 time == 6 beats per bar/eighth note beats

These are just a few time signatures that you can utilize, practice with a metronome to strengthen your sense of timing.
Furthermore, practice stressing a specific beat in all bars to hear how it changes the way the music feels.
Lastly, you will find different ways of counting time, by this I mean that you might not count one, two, ... six, instead
you might count One and a Two and a, One etc.. Be open to learning and practice everyday.
