Lydian Mode

Fourth Mode, it is worth noting that since Lydian is only a semitone away from phrygian, the patterns
will look extremely similar. In fact we will not show the combined modes on this one
because the pattern at the end of phrygian includes the Lydian pattern.

Lydian Mode

Lydian Mode: 1 - 2 - 3 - [#4] - 5 - 6 - 7
Unique Note: #4

The Lydian Mode allows us to start and end on the fourth note in any Key. In this case
it would be F in the key of C.

example: F - G - A - Bb - C - D - E - F == F Ionian
F - G - A - B - C - D - E - F == F Lydian
