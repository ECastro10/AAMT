Major Pentatonic Scale

First and foremost, let's talk about what it means to be a pentatonic scale.

Pentatonic

So as you probably noticed, the major and minor scales had 7 notes before reaching the higher octave root.
Those 7 note scales are known as heptatonic scales.
Now if you use the root of pentatonic == "penta", you can arrive at the conclusion that it means five notes.
If you did not, penta means 5 (like the pentagon), and pentatonic means 5 notes.
The basic gist of pentatonic scales is to remove any neighboring tones or tones that are separated by a
half step or semitone. Therefore we have two types of pentatonic scales, major and minor.
Knowing that we only need 5 of the 7 notes in both the major and minor scales, let's move on to our first
pentatonic scale.

Major Pentatonic Scale

Let's talk about the C major pentatonic scale.
The reason we want to learn these 5 note scales is because by removing neighboring tones we remove a possibility
of playing a dissonant note (the most dissonant note combinations are those that are only a semitone apart).
Also, pentatonic scales are just about worldwide, many cultures have utilized the pentatonic scale, whether they
called it that or not. It is just an easy go to when you don't know what notes to play.

In the key of C we have the following notes: C, D, E, F, G, A, B
We know that the two pairs of neighboring tones are E - F and B - C.

When making any major pentatonic scale, we remove the 4th and 7th scale degree from the original major scale.
As a result we are left with 1, 2, 3, 5, 6 == C, D, E, G, A --> we removed the F and the C. Therefore our formula looks like this:
