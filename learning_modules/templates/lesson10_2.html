Harmonic Minor Scale

Let's dive into the harmonic minor scale

The image above shows the A harmonic minor scale up and down one octave but it captures the essence of
sharpening that 7th degree in our scale.
Now let's see how a two octave pattern looks like on the guitar fretboard.

If you haven't already noticed, this is basically the natural minor scale with a sharpened 7th == #7.
This raised 7th gives it an almost a foreign sound.
We raise that 7th so that it sounds more harmonious with dominant chords which we will cover next chapter.
Recall how the major scale has this single semitone difference between the 7th and the Root.
To maneuver through this scale requires only one shift.
Do the shift however you want but make sure it happens in a way that allows you to play the top three strings (G, B, E)
in one position. And remember, one finger per note, at least for now.
