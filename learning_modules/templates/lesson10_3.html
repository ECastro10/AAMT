Melodic Minor Scale

One of the final variations of the minor scale is known as the Melodic Minor Scale.
This is a whole new level of playing scale.
The concept is to play up the scale one way and play down the scale another.
This ideology stems from the need to play a certain scale on the upbeat and then a different scale
on the downbeat.

NOTES: As mentioned before all of our patterns so far have been two octave patterns,
you can play up one octave and then switch to the minor while continuing up,
you can play up one octave and then back down that same octave in a minor fashion,
or you can play two octaves in the modified manor and back down in a minor for two octaves.
It is entirely up to you but I think the chords being played in behind you will dictate how long
you can play either of the scales in the melodic minor scale.
Remember, practice, practice, practice.
