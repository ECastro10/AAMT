Tones versus Semitones

So in the last lesson we learned about the chromatic scale and how it basically just goes to the next note.
From here on out our scales will have patterns but in order to understand that you need to understand
the difference between a Tone and a Semitone.

Difference between a Tone and a Semitone

Remember the image of the piano keys? If not, here you go.

Let's focus on the natural notes (A, B, C, D, E, F, G)
Every space between notes is called a half step. Know this word because we will be using it from now on.
So C --> C#/Db is considered a half step. Now what if you want to go from C --> D? It can be two half steps or
we can call it by it's name, a Whole Step. Remember this name as well.
So D --> E is a whole step but E --> F is only a half step. Why do B --> C and E --> F have no accidentals?
That is another discussion that we will tackle later on.
