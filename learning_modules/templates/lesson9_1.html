Chord Sequences aka "chord progressions"

Before we dive into any chord sequences, let's talk about why we use diatonic chord sequences.
Diatonic means within a key or scale
Songs have a general chord sequences to that they adhere to, this means that they will repeat this chord
sequence however many times until they reach a break in between the sequence or the end of the song.
These are not the end all be all because you can modulate between keys which means changing keys (we won't discuss in this chapter).
Explore as many of these sequences as you can in all keys to hear what the sequences sound like.

Chord Sequences

In terms of how to play them, play them the same way you played the cadences.

Set your metronome at a comfortable speed and practice in 4/4 time at first, playing each chord for one bar.
You can also look up other chord progressions if you'd like.
