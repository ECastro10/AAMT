Suspended Chords

For suspended chords you have to remember one more interval, the perfect 4th.
Remember how the 3rd dictated whether a chord was major or minor? Well with suspended chords you tamper with the 3rd.
Let's dive in.

Suspended chords formula

There two basic kinds of suspended chords: the suspended 4th (sus4) and the suspended 2nd (sus2).
The reason they are suspended is because we change the major 3rd to a perfect 4th or major 2nd.

Suspended 4th

FORMULA: 1 - 4 - 5 In order to achieve the suspended 4th all you have to do is sharpen the 3rd in a major chord == major 3rd -- > perfect 4th.
Or double sharpen the 3rd in a minor chord == minor 3rd -- > major 3rd -- > perfect 4th.
Very simply move, just move the finger that is on the 3rd up one fret.

Suspended 2nd

FORMULA: 1 - 2 - 5 In order to achieve the suspended 2nd you have to double flatten the 3rd in a major chord == major 3rd --> minor 3rd --> major 2nd.
Or simply flatten the third in a minor chord == minor 3rd --> major 2nd.

Both of these chords give you the suspended feel and sound because they are neither major nor minor.
Play with suspended chords, you will find that they can expand your vocabulary of sound when playing just a major or minor chord.
